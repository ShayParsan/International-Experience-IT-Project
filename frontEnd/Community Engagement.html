Volunteer Stories

Meet the passionate individuals who have volunteered their time and resources to help manage water hyacinths. Discover how their efforts are helping protect Hartebeespoort Dam for future generations.

Volunteer Story: Thabo’s First Cleanup Experience

“I didn’t expect one day of volunteering to change my perspective.”

Thabo Mokoena, a student from Pretoria, joined his first hyacinth cleanup at Hartebeespoort Dam after seeing a flyer on campus. He spent the day alongside dozens of volunteers removing invasive hyacinths and learning about their environmental impact.

“It was hard work, but seeing the difference we made was amazing.”

The event sparked a passion in Thabo—he’s since started an environmental club at his university and encourages others to join future cleanups.

“Even one day can make a big impact.”


Volunteer Story: Lerato’s Family Day at the Dam

“It was the best family day we’ve had in years.”

Lerato Dlamini, a mother of two from Hartbeespoort, brought her family to the cleanup event for something fun and meaningful. Her children, aged 8 and 10, loved getting muddy while helping to clear hyacinths.

“It taught them the value of nature and teamwork,” Lerato said.

They left the event tired but proud, already planning to return for the next one.

“It’s now a tradition for us.”


Volunteer Story: Ahmed’s Unexpected Passion

“I signed up on a whim—now I can’t stop!”

Ahmed Khan, an IT technician from Johannesburg, wasn’t sure what to expect when he joined a cleanup effort with his friends. But after a few hours of hauling hyacinths and chatting with other volunteers, he was hooked.

“I realized how much I love being outdoors and helping the environment.”

Since then, Ahmed has attended three more events and even helped build a tracking app for cleanup progress.

“Volunteering gave me purpose beyond my 9–5.”
