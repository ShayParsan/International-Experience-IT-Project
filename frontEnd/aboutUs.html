The problem

The proliferation of water hyacinth in Hartbeespoort has become a serious problem in the dam and other bodies of water around South Africa. The invasive species has been spreading widely since its first introduction to the continent and is taking over and eliminating several ecological niches. The main issues with the plant are its ability to grow in large patches and cover big areas of the lake’s surface. This blocks waterways, limits boat traffic and covers the underlying ecosystem in darkness. Furthermore, due to the dense matting of hyacinth, the chemical composition of the water gets altered as well. The main threat of the invasive species is twofold: the plants are destroying ecosystems and reducing water access for people living near the dam.

Our Goal

We created this website with the intension to provide a company with the interface to easily:

  • Attract community interested.
  • Educate the community on the urgancy of the situation.
  • Provide the community with an easy way to view hyacinth data.
  • Show hyacinth predictions.
  • Recruit and manage comunity members who wish to help aid in the hyacinth removal.
